Materialism

Material-Empirical
Foundation: matter

Reality is fundamentally physical matter and energy. Ethical value derives from material wellbeing, physical autonomy, and empirical consequences.

Sensationalism

Material-Empirical
Foundation: sensation

Knowledge and value derive from sensory experience. Ethics focuses on pleasure, pain, and empirically observable welfare.

Phenomenalism

Material-Empirical
Foundation: phenomena

Reality consists of observable phenomena. Ethical judgment is based on experiential consequences and phenomenological analysis.

Realism

Material-Empirical
Foundation: external_reality

An objective external reality exists independent of perception. Ethics grounded in objective facts about wellbeing and harm.

Dynamism

Process-Individual
Foundation: becoming

Reality is fundamentally process and change. Ethics emphasizes growth, transformation, and dynamic balance.

Monadism

Process-Individual
Foundation: individual_monad

Each being is a unique, irreducible individual. Ethics centers on individual autonomy, uniqueness, and self-determination.

Idealism

Process-Individual
Foundation: consciousness

Mind and consciousness are primary. Ethical value derives from conscious experience, intention, and mental states.

Rationalism

Process-Individual
Foundation: reason

Reason is the foundation of knowledge and value. Ethics based on logical consistency, rational principles, and universal laws.

Psychism

Depth-Spiritual
Foundation: psyche

The psyche and unconscious depths shape experience. Ethics involves archetypal patterns, symbolic meaning, and psychological wholeness.

Pneumatism

Depth-Spiritual
Foundation: living_spirit

Living spirit permeates all existence (animism). Ethics respects ensouled nature, spirits, and the web of life.

Spiritualism

Depth-Spiritual
Foundation: transcendent_spirit

Transcendent divine spirit is ultimate reality. Ethics derives from sacred principles, divine will, and spiritual truth.

Mathematism

Depth-Spiritual
Foundation: mathematical_form

Mathematical forms and structures are fundamental. Ethics based on harmony, proportion, and formal beauty (Platonic).

How Worldviews Work in Deliberation

1. Multi-Perspectival Analysis

Each worldview evaluates your scenario independently, providing its own judgment, confidence level, and reasoning.

2. Domain Weighting

Worldviews are weighted differently depending on the domain (e.g., Materialism has high weight in healthcare, Spiritualism in spiritual domains).

3. Conflict Resolution

When worldviews disagree, the 7-step integration procedure identifies conflicts, applies domain weights, and synthesizes an integrated judgment.

4. Minority View Preservation

Dissenting perspectives are always preserved and presented alongside the integrated judgment, ensuring epistemic humility.
